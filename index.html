Welcome to Pitch and Putt Pro

Definition

Pitch and putt is an amateur sport very similar to, and derived from, golf, where the hole length is typically up to 100 yd and just 2–3 clubs are normally used. The game was organised and developed in Ireland during the early 20th century, before expanding through the 1940s, and is now played in dozens of countries.

History

Pitch and putt originated in Ireland in the early 20th century as a shorter and more accessible version of golf. The sport gained popularity in the 1940s and has since spread to many countries worldwide, with various national and international governing bodies established to oversee its development.
